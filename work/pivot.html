As a member of Berkeley Innovation, a human-centered design club at UC Berkeley, I worked with three other students (Ji-hern Baek, Nickki Nguyen, and Kylon Chiang) on a project with Vertigo Software in the fall of 2015. We were assigned to design and prototype what is essentially Git for designers. A list of Git-equivalent features, including branching and the ability to resolve merge conflicts, were given to us, but the design and function of the application was largely up to us. While all four of us worked equally on ideation, wireframing, and the visual design of our project, we each were in charge of the design of different pages as we moved toward higher fidelity mockups. I was responsible for the pages you see here: the revision conflict page and the page for a single revision of a file.
